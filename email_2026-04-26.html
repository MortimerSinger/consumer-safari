Meta announces 8,000 layoffs (10% of workforce); pulls 6,000 open requisitions
CNBC
Memo cites efficiency and rebalancing toward AI infrastructure. Layoffs begin May 20. The 6,000 cancelled open roles is the more telling number, net hiring cessation, not just headcount cut.
Microsoft offers buyouts to 7% of U.S. workforce, first voluntary buyouts in 51 years
CNBC
Targets longer-tenured employees among ~125,000 U.S. staff, implying ~8,750 potential cuts. First-ever voluntary buyout program signals structural cost reset, not cyclical trim.
Tech sector layoffs cross 92,000 in 2026 YTD; Mag-4 to spend $700B on AI infrastructure
CNBC
Hiring chill is most acute for generalized IT and entry-level roles. AI-native positions still in demand. The infrastructure spend is what justifies the labor reset.

1.5% of total retail. Microsoft data: Copilot users 53% more likely to purchase within 30 minutes; 194% more likely with shopping intent. AI traffic converts at 38% higher rate than traditional channels.

73% of consumers already using AI in shopping, 45% for product ideas, 37% for review summaries. Zero-click commerce, purchases without ever visiting a website, is now the disruptive scenario.
