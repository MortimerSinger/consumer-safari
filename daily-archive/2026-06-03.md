_Brief dated: Wednesday, June 3, 2026_

## Today's Read

Big M&A print on Monday: Hims & Hers closed its acquisition of Eucalyptus, the parent of Juniper, Pilot, Kin, and Software, instantly becoming the world's largest consumer health platform and entering Australia, the UK, Germany, Japan, and Canada in a single move (Eucalyptus had facilitated ~2M consultations). Combined with last week's Church & Dwight buy of Miss Mouth's Messy Eater ($325M, ~$80M TTM revenue, ~$28M EBITDA), the playbook is clear: scale platforms are paying premium multiples for digitally native, category-defining DTC brands. Macro picture continues to bifurcate: April retail sales rose +0.5% MoM ($757.1B) but consumer sentiment hit a historic low of 44.8, and recent retail earnings show off-price/value formats accelerating while discretionary names struggle. The agentic commerce arms race is now production-grade: Amazon is productizing its Rufus AI shopping stack for retailers via AWS, and Mirakl's marketplace data shows agent-ready commerce growing GMV 31% YoY (~4.5x traditional eCom).

### Patterns
- **Scale platforms buying digitally native brands at premium multiples** — Hims paid for a global health platform (5-country expansion in one deal) rather than building it. Church & Dwight paid ~4x revenue and ~12x EBITDA for Miss Mouth's. The arbitrage: large CPG/consumer health buyers can afford category-leader multiples because they unlock distribution leverage the founders couldn't. Watch for more $200M-$500M tuck-ins targeting DTC brands with category dominance and clean unit economics.
- **Macro print is still mixed, but the surveys are cratering** — Hard numbers (April retail +0.5% MoM, April core CPI 2.8% YoY, retail earnings beats from Dollar Tree/Burlington) say the consumer is holding. Soft numbers (UMich 44.8 historic low, Conference Board 93.1, Iran/gas anxiety) say the consumer is breaking. The bifurcation that actually matters: value/off-price/discount formats are taking share from premium and discretionary across virtually every category.
- **Agentic commerce moves from theory to retailer P&L** — Amazon offering AWS-packaged AI shopping (Rufus stack) to non-Amazon retailers turns the agentic layer into a procurement decision rather than an R&D bet. Mirakl is publishing 31% GMV growth as the proof point that marketplaces with clean machine-readable data are getting recommended by agents. The defensive moat for incumbents: clean product data, structured assortment, direct LLM integration. The offensive opportunity: AI-native startups can now stand up agent-visible commerce in 60 days.
